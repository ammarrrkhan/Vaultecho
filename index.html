Flood Safety Guide
Before a Flood:
  • Know your area's flood risk and elevation
  • Prepare an emergency kit with food, water, and medications
  • Keep important documents in waterproof containers
During a Flood:
  • Move to higher ground immediately
  • Avoid walking or driving through flood waters
  • Turn off utilities at main switches if instructed
After a Flood:
  • Return home only when authorities say it's safe
  • Be aware of areas where floodwaters have receded
  • Clean and disinfect everything that got wet
Cyber Scam Protection Guide
Identifying Scams:
  • Unexpected requests for personal information
  • Urgent or threatening language in messages
  • Links to unfamiliar websites
  • Misspellings or poor grammar in official-looking communications
Protective Measures:
  • Enable two-factor authentication on all accounts
  • Use unique passwords for different services
  • Verify unexpected requests through official channels
  • Keep software and antivirus programs updated
Heatwave Survival Guide
Staying Cool:
  • Stay in air-conditioned buildings as much as possible
  • Use fans to circulate air if AC isn't available
  • Take cool showers or baths to lower body temperature
  • Wear lightweight, light-colored, loose-fitting clothing
Hydration:
  • Drink more water than usual
  • Avoid alcohol and high-sugar drinks
  • Eat small, frequent meals
  • Monitor urine color - pale yellow means proper hydration
Earthquake Safety Guide
During an Earthquake:
  • Drop, Cover, and Hold On under sturdy furniture
  • Stay indoors until shaking stops
  • Stay away from windows, glass, and heavy objects
  • If outside, move to an open area away from buildings
After an Earthquake:
  • Check for injuries and provide first aid
  • Inspect utilities for damage
  • Be prepared for aftershocks
  • Listen to emergency broadcasts for information
Wildfire Safety Guide
Preparation:
  • Create a defensible space around your home
  • Prepare an evacuation plan with multiple routes
  • Assemble emergency supplies including N95 masks
  • Know your community's evacuation plans
During a Wildfire:
  • Evacuate immediately if ordered
  • Wear protective clothing if outside
  • Close all windows and doors before leaving
  • Follow designated evacuation routes
Hurricane Preparedness Guide
Before the Storm:
  • Board up windows or install storm shutters
  • Secure outdoor objects that could become projectiles
  • Stock up on non-perishable food and water
  • Charge all electronic devices
During the Hurricane:
  • Stay indoors away from windows and glass doors
  • Take refuge in a small interior room on lowest level
  • Use flashlights rather than candles
  • Monitor emergency broadcasts
