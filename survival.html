Essential Survival Tips

Flip each card to discover vital survival techniques for different emergency scenarios

Ocean Survival

Stranded at sea? Learn what to do

Ocean Survival

  • Stay with your boat or raft if possible
  • Conserve energy and body heat - avoid unnecessary movement
  • Ration food and water immediately
  • Protect yourself from sun exposure
  • Signal for help using mirrors, flares, or bright clothing
  • Don't drink seawater - it accelerates dehydration

Fire Safety

Escaping a burning building

Fire Safety

  • Check doors for heat before opening - use the back of your hand
  • Stay low to avoid smoke inhalation
  • Use stairs, never elevators during a fire
  • If trapped, seal doors with wet towels and signal from windows
  • Stop, drop, and roll if your clothes catch fire
  • Have an escape plan with two exits from every room

Earthquake

When the ground starts shaking

Earthquake Safety

  • Drop, cover, and hold on under sturdy furniture
  • Stay indoors until shaking stops
  • Stay away from windows, glass, and exterior walls
  • If outside, move to an open area away from buildings
  • If driving, pull over and stop in a clear area
  • Expect aftershocks and be prepared

Wilderness

Lost in the wild? Stay alive

Wilderness Survival

  • Remember the rule of threes: 3 minutes without air, 3 hours without shelter, 3 days without water, 3 weeks without food
  • Prioritize shelter, water, fire, then food
  • Stay put if you're lost to increase chances of being found
  • Purify water by boiling or using purification tablets
  • Learn to identify edible plants in your area

First Aid

Emergency medical response

First Aid Basics

  • ABCs: Check Airway, Breathing, and Circulation first
  • Stop bleeding with direct pressure and elevation
  • Treat for shock by keeping the person warm and elevating legs
  • Learn CPR and the Heimlich maneuver
  • Immobilize suspected fractures
  • Know how to recognize and treat heat stroke and hypothermia

Self-Defense

Protecting yourself from attack

Self-Defense Tips

  • Awareness is your first defense - avoid dangerous situations
  • Trust your instincts - if something feels wrong, act immediately
  • Make noise to attract attention if threatened
  • Target vulnerable areas: eyes, nose, throat, groin, knees
  • Use everyday objects as improvised weapons
  • Escape is always the priority, not fighting

Flood Safety

Surviving rising waters

Flood Safety

  • Move to higher ground immediately if flooding occurs
  • Never walk or drive through floodwaters - just 6 inches can knock you down
  • Avoid contact with floodwater as it may be contaminated
  • Turn off electricity at the main breaker if water enters your home
  • Have an evacuation plan and know your community's flood warning system

Extreme Cold

Surviving freezing temperatures

Extreme Cold Survival

  • Layer clothing - wool and synthetics retain heat when wet
  • Protect extremities - most heat loss occurs through head, hands and feet
  • Stay dry - wet clothing loses insulation value rapidly
  • Watch for signs of frostbite and hypothermia
  • Keep moving to generate body heat, but avoid sweating
  • Eat high-energy foods and stay hydrated

Power Outage

Living without electricity

Power Outage Tips

  • Keep refrigerator and freezer doors closed to preserve food
  • Use flashlights instead of candles to reduce fire risk
  • Turn off and unplug electronics to protect from surges
  • Keep a battery-powered radio for emergency information
  • Have alternative cooking methods available
  • Know how to manually open your garage door

Chemical Exposure

Handling hazardous materials

Chemical Exposure

  • Move to fresh air immediately if exposed to chemicals
  • Remove contaminated clothing and rinse skin with water
  • Flush eyes with clean water for 15-20 minutes if exposed
  • Don't induce vomiting unless directed by poison control
  • Have emergency numbers (like poison control) readily available
  • Store chemicals properly and keep MSDS sheets accessible

Active Shooter

Responding to violence

Active Shooter Response

  • Run: Have an escape route in mind, leave belongings behind
  • Hide: If you can't escape, hide in a secure location
  • Fight: As a last resort, attempt to incapacitate the shooter
  • Silence your phone and remain quiet if hiding
  • When law enforcement arrives, keep hands visible and follow instructions
  • Know your workplace/school's emergency procedures

Pandemic

Staying safe during outbreaks

Pandemic Preparedness

  • Wash hands frequently with soap and water
  • Maintain social distancing during outbreaks
  • Have a 2-week supply of food and medications
  • Stay informed through reliable health sources
  • Follow vaccination recommendations
  • Have a plan for childcare if schools close

Survival FAQ


Answers to common wilderness survival questions

The survival rule of 3 states:

  • 3 minutes without air
  • 3 hours without shelter in harsh conditions
  • 3 days without water
  • 3 weeks without food

These priorities guide your survival strategy in wilderness emergencies.

Several methods exist for emergency water purification:

  • Boiling: Roll boil for 1 minute (3 mins at high altitude)
  • Solar disinfection: Clear plastic bottles in direct sunlight for 6 hours
  • Improvised filtration: Layer sand, charcoal, and cloth in a container
  • Natural indicators: Clear flowing water is generally safer than stagnant water

Effective signaling techniques include:

  • Visual signals: Create large SOS symbols (9ft tall) using rocks or logs
  • Mirror flashes: Reflect sunlight with any shiny surface (3 flashes = distress)
  • Smoke signals: Create thick smoke by adding green vegetation to fire
  • Auditory signals: Three loud noises (whistles, gunshots, or shouts) in succession

Basic shelter principles:

  • Location: Dry ground, away from hazards, near resources
  • Insulation: Use leaves, pine needles, or grass between shelter layers
  • Types:
    • Lean-to (against fallen tree or rock face)
    • A-frame (between two trees with ridge pole)
    • Snow trench (in winter conditions)
  • Size: Just big enough to fit your body to retain heat

Essential survival kit items:

  • Fire starter (lighter, ferro rod)
  • Water purification tablets
  • Compact emergency blanket
  • Signal mirror
  • Paracord (50+ feet)
  • First aid supplies
  • Multi-tool or knife
  • High-calorie energy bars
