GET TO KNOW US

 

Vaultecho is a dedicated safety awareness website designed for all age groups, aiming to educate and empower individuals with essential survival tips and emergency response tools. Founded by passionate students Ammar Ahmed Khan and Pariwash Khan, Vaultecho was born from the belief that safety is a fundamental human right. The platform is committed to spreading knowledge that can save lives—because everyone deserves to feel prepared, protected, and informed in times of crisis.

Our Story

Vaultecho began as a shared vision between two passionate students — Ammar Ahmed Khan and Pariwash Khan — who recognized a critical gap in accessible safety awareness. With equal dedication, they combined their skills in technology and design to create a platform that empowers people of all ages with survival tips, emergency response tools, and real-world safety guidance.

Driven by the belief that safety is every human’s right, they transformed a simple idea into a powerful resource. From long nights of planning and coding to refining the smallest design details, both Ammar and Pariwash poured equal effort into shaping Vaultecho — not just as a website, but as a mission to protect, educate, and inspire.

Our first confirmed life-saving story - September 19, 2020

"That alarm was the difference between life and death"

At 2:14 AM on September 19, 2020, our seismic sensors detected what would become a 7.1 magnitude earthquake. Twelve seconds before the shaking began, an emergency alert blasted from the Rodriguez family's phones at 110 decibels - loud enough to wake them from deep sleep.

As the power failed and buildings began crumbling around them, Maria Rodriguez followed the flashing red instructions on her VaultEcho app: "EARTHQUAKE! MOVE TO MARKED SAFE ZONE NOW." The app's pre-identified structural safety points guided the family of five to the reinforced doorway just as the first shockwaves hit.

When rescue teams arrived at dawn, they found the Rodriguez family unharmed in their designated safe zone, while neighboring apartments had collapsed. Fire Captain Esteban Morales would later tell reporters: "That app gave them the critical seconds they needed. I've never seen technology make such a direct life-or-death difference."

Turning silence into signal. Protecting human lives through intelligent emergency response.
