Community Safety Tips & Tricks

Practical advice from our community members that you can use in your daily life.

Travel

Public Transport Awareness

When using public transport at night, always sit near the driver or in view of CCTV cameras. Have your transit card ready before boarding to avoid fumbling with your wallet in vulnerable situations.

TravelSafe22 June 3, 2023
Digital

Digital Footprint Cleanup

Every 3 months, search your name online and remove any personal information you find. Set up Google Alerts for your name to monitor new appearances. Use privacy-checking tools to audit your social media settings.

Anonymous Hidden May 28, 2023
Home

Emergency Kit Hack

Keep a small emergency kit in your car and office with these essentials: protein bars, water pouches, flashlight, power bank, emergency blanket, and a written list of emergency contacts (in case your phone dies).

PreparedParent May 15, 2023
Public

Walking Safety Technique

When walking alone, hold your keys between your fingers (like makeshift claws) and have your phone ready with emergency services pre-dialed (just don't press call). Walk with purpose and make occasional eye contact with passersby to show awareness.

UrbanSafetyPro April 30, 2023
Travel

Hotel Safety Check

When checking into a hotel, always locate the emergency exits and count the doors to your room from the exit. Place a door wedge alarm (or even a glass against the door handle) to alert you if someone tries to enter while you're sleeping.

FrequentTraveler April 22, 2023
Transport

Rideshare Verification

Before entering a rideshare, verify the license plate, car model, and driver photo. Ask the driver "Who are you here for?" instead of confirming your name. Sit in the back seat behind the driver for easiest exit if needed.

Anonymous Hidden April 10, 2023
Digital

Password Security

Use a password manager to generate and store unique passwords. Enable two-factor authentication everywhere possible. Never reuse passwords across sites. Check haveibeenpwned.com periodically to see if your accounts have been compromised.

CyberGuard March 28, 2023
Home

Fire Safety Check

Test smoke alarms monthly and replace batteries yearly. Keep fire extinguishers in kitchen and garage. Practice escape routes with family. Never leave candles unattended. Keep flammable items away from heat sources.

SafetyFirst March 15, 2023
Public

Parking Lot Awareness

Park near lights and security cameras when possible. Have keys ready before approaching your car. Check backseat before entering. If feeling unsafe, request security escort. Avoid parking next to vans or large vehicles that could block visibility.

AwarenessAdvocate February 22, 2023
Travel

International Travel Prep

Scan passport and important documents to cloud storage. Register with embassy. Learn emergency numbers for destination. Carry local currency in multiple places. Know location of nearest hospital. Share itinerary with trusted contacts back home.

GlobalTraveler February 8, 2023
